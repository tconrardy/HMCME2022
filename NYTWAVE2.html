Ukrainian Accession to NATO Postponed Following Sabatoge of Underwater High-Speed Cables by Russian Submarines

NATO agreed to expedited accession talks in the wake of a Russian tank exercise in Ukranian territory last week, but Russian tactics have sown renewed uncertainty among Western diplomats.

NATO scrapped plans to discuss accession with Ukraine today and instead met to discuss the threat of Russian internet cable sabatoge and continued military buildup.

By Moe Kittridge

Jan. 21, 2022

Billions of internet users expressed frustration this morning when they found that their internet had slowed significantly; unbeknownst to these billions of web surfers, this buffering was the result of a complex strategy of hybrid warfare that the Russian government has adapted in the face of challenges from NATO.

Following a border skirmish last week involving 12 Russian tanks, NATO member states voted to initiate expedited accession talks with Ukraine. Ukraine quickly drafted a letter of intent to NATO and submitted reform timelines as required by the accession process; the agenda of today's scheduled NATO meeting was for existing members to sign Ukraine's acession protocols. Today's meeting, however, was cancelled following military and intelligence reports that Russia had used advanced military submarines to cut two major high speed underwater cables.

While intelligence as to where Russian submarines severed the cables, US intelligence has confirmed that Russian submarines severed both the Marea and Durant cables.

The two cables affected—the Marea and Dunant cables—are two of the newest and highest-capacity underwater high-speed cables in the world, boasting capacities of 200 Tbit/s and 250 Tbit/s, respectively. Each cable also links the US to a NATO ally. The Marea cable, installed in 2018 by Facebook, Microsoft, and Telxius, runs between the United states and Spain. The Dunant cable, which owner Google first sent live in 2020, runs between the US and France. While the severing of these cables is particularly hard on American and European internet speeds, these cable outages will impact internet traffic worldwide. Critically, the outage as it stands only poses an inconvenience, as opposed to a significant security threat, but the message is clear: NATO noncompliance with Russian demands will not go unanswered.

While military communications between the US and NATO allies went largely uninterupted, American and European markets took drastic dips amidst confusion during the outages. Representatives from Google, Microsoft, and Facebook reportedly met with President Biden and military leaders earlier today to discuss the security of other underwater cables and to help reassure industry leaders that the US will prevent further disruptions.

Russia has yet to respond to allegations of cable sabatoge, and NATO has yet to announce whether it will proceed with accession talks for Ukraine following the cancellation of today's scheduled protocol signing.
